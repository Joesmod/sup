The Department of Labor's website discusses employment protections for workers who are pregnant or nursing. These protections exist in 46 states and the District of Columbia, and they prohibit discrimination against pregnant or nursing workers. The protections vary from state to state, but they typically guarantee employment leave for pregnancy and birth, and provide coverage for pregnancy-related services under health insurance plans. Some states also have "safe haven" laws that allow women to anonymously drop off newborn babies.
